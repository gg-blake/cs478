Review Board (IRB) Research involving human participants must be reviewed and approved by the UMass Boston Institutional Review Board (IRB) before the study begins. The IRB is an independent ethics committee established to determine if human research studies follow the ethical principles and federal regulations with the primary goal of protecting human participants. The IRB has the authority to approve, disapprove or require modifications of these studies. All PIs and research personnel must follow the requirements in the Investigator Manual (HRP-103) .
