Additional Information Victoria Weston is a specialist in how traditional schools of Japanese painting engaged the positions of indigenous versus foreign. Weston focuses on Tokyo painters aligned with the art critic and educator Okakura Kakuzo, including Yokoyama Taikan, Hishida Shunso, and Shimomura Kanzan. These figures traveled and exhibited in Europe and America in the early 1900s; Weston conducts archival research on their activities in the American Northeast. Additionally, Weston is an active curator of exhibitions addressing Japanese art and the West.
