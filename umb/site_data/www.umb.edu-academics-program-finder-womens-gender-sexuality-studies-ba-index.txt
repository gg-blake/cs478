Discover how gender shapes our experiences, institutions, and social realities. Discover the academic frameworks behind empowerment and social change with the Women’s, Gender, and Sexuality Studies BA at UMass Boston. Explore the rich history, diverse experiences, and critical issues surrounding gender, sexuality, and women's rights, and develop a deep understanding of intersectionality, social justice, and feminist theory. In this program, you will: Develop a comprehensive understanding of the historical, social, cultural, and political factors that shape the experiences and perspectives of women, fostering critical thinking and analytical skills Explore the complexities of gender, sexuality, race, class, and other intersecting identities, gaining a nuanced understanding of power dynamics, inequalities, and social justice issues Cultivate effective communication, advocacy, and leadership skills, empowering you to engage in social activism, community organizing, and policy-making to advance gender equity and social change Career Possibilities Work with organizations or government agencies to promote inclusivity, gender equity, and diversity through policy development. Manage and coordinate programs and initiatives focused on women's empowerment in the nonprofit sector. Or, report on gender-related issues, women's rights movements, and social justice topics for media outlets, magazines, or online platforms. These are just a few of the possibilities. See graduation requirements for more information. University Requirements College of Liberal Arts Requirements for Bachelor of Arts Major Requirements Complete at least 30 credits from ten courses. Residency: At least six courses for the major must be taken through the Women’s and Gender Studies Department. At least five courses for the major must be completed at UMass Boston. Course level: At least fifteen credits for the major must be at the 300-level or higher. Course limits: No more than two independent study courses may be applied toward the major.
