It will be updated quarterly. Both the university and our students have experienced added expenses and revenue losses due to the impacts of COVID-19. Some financial support to offset these impacts has been granted by both the federal and state governments to address the needs of students and the university.  The campus has received grant awards from several sources totaling approximately $77.3 million (to-date) to aid in addressing the unexpected costs of COVID-direct impacts (such as emergency response and COVID testing), to support students and the university in providing remote instruction, and to address student and university resource loss. There are five main sources of funds active to date, including FEMA, CARES, GEER, CRRSAA and ARPA. In the below table, you will find details of each of these sources and how the university is employing them. Total Aid Awarded: $77,298,556. Note that HEERF below stands for Higher Education Emergency Relief Fund. Funding Sources Federal Emergency Management Agency (FEMA) Source of Aid: Federal Emergency Management Agency (FEMA) Type of Aid: Eligible Cost Reimbursement Short Definition of Funding: Emergency Response (food, shelter, campus safety) and administrative costs up to a maximum % of total submission Award Amount: 75% of eligible costs submitted and approved. 100% of administrative costs. Current Status (7/10/22): Campus has submitted $142K of expenses for reimbursement; $128K in expenses have been approved.
