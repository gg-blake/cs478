CED or WES evaluation for foreign coursework $60 application fee Test of Essential Academic Skills (TEAS) scores are optional If provided, exam results will be included in the application review process If submitting TEAS scores, we recommend a 75 or higher on each section We will accept all of the following TEAS: TEAS at PSI, TEAS at ATI, Remote proctored TEAS Two Short Essays 500-Word Essay : UMass Boston's Manning College of Nursing and Health Sciences (MCNHS) is committed to advance efforts towards promoting health equity, social justice, and population-based health in our community and beyond by educating health professionals to serve communities as diverse as our students. ​Why are you interested in studying nursing at UMass Boston, an institution committed to advancing health equity? 250-Word Essay : UMass Boston’s Accelerated Nursing program is designed to blend high quality clinical experiences with online didactic learning. Successful students must be flexible, adaptable, and self-directed. Please describe a time when you independently learned a complex task. What was the task? What resources did you utilize to learn it? What did you learn about yourself during this process? Two Recommendation Letters Accelerated Nursing Prerequisite Form - will be made available in Beacon Gateway upon application submission Admission Criteria Required cumulative GPA of 3.0 (includes all college-level institutions attended) Required prerequisite GPA of 3.3 Prerequisite Courses The following courses must be a C or higher: Intro Sociology or Intro Psychology (3 credits) Statistics (3 credits) The following courses must be a C+ or higher: Human Growth & Development (3 credits) Nutrition (3 credits) The following courses must be a C+ or higher, include a lab, and be completed within the last 10 years: Anatomy & Physiology I (4 credits) Anatomy & Physiology II (4 credits) Microbiology (4 credits) Check out the Accelerated Nursing Prerequisite Guide.pdf to confirm your courses fulfill the requirements.
