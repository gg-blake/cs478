Storage Precautions Store in airtight containers in a dark, cool, dry place. Label containers with receiving, opening and disposal dates. Dispose of peroxide forming chemicals before expected date of first peroxide formation in accordance with local regulations. Test for the presence of peroxides periodically. Acetaldehyde Acrylaldehyde Crotonaldehyde Cyclohexane Ethyl Ether Isopropyl Ether p-Dioxane Potassium Tetrahydrofuran * Potassium peroxide often exists in the crust around a piece of Potassium. When cut with a knife the peroxide rapidly oxidizes the residual kerosene resulting in an explosion.
