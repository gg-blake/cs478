Degrees MEd, Bilingual/ESL Studies, University of Massachusetts MA, English Literature, University of Massachusetts BA, English/Political Science, Boston College Professional Publications & Contributions “Civic Engagement and the Many Faces of Change,” report for the Civic Engaged Scholars Initiative (CESI), 2017 Community-University Project for Literacy, UMB Office of Community Partnerships Poster, Scholar Works, 2015 “Building on Community Strengths: A Model for Training,” with Auerbach, Arnaud and Zombrano in Adult ESL: Politics, Pedagogy, and Participation in Classroom and Community Programs, Trudy Smoke, editor, 1998 “Community-Building: One Tutor at a Time,” The Change Agent: An Adult Education Magazine for Social Justice, World Education, 1997 “We Need a New Definition of Adult Education,” The Change Agent: An Adult Education Magazine for Social Justice, World Education, 1997 Additional Information Carol Chandler-Rourke has been teaching writing as well as tutor training and graduate teacher-training courses at UMass/Boston since 1984 and has more than three decades of experience in community development, civic engagement and developing community-university partnerships. Formerly the Deputy Director of the Haitian Multi-Service Center in Dorchester (1984-1996) and Director of Community Building at the Massachusetts state Office for Refugees and Immigrants (ORI) (1997-2013), Carol’s work has focused on creating leadership opportunities for immigrants and refugees, developing community-university partnerships and strengthening community-based organizations at the national, state and grassroots level. Since 1989, Carol has coordinated the Community University Project for Literacy (CUPL) at UMB.
